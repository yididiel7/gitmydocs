Getting votes from poor or economically disadvantaged communities often involves complex dynamics:

1. Campaign Promises:
- Politicians frequently promise social programs, minimum wage increases, and better public services
- However, these promises aren't always fulfilled after elections

2. Economic Messaging:
- Focus on jobs, healthcare, education, and economic mobility
- Appeals to immediate needs and struggles of lower-income voters

3. Ethical Concerns:
- Risk of exploitation through empty promises
- Potential manipulation of economic anxieties
- Question of authentic representation vs opportunistic vote-seeking

4. Practical Issues:
- Lower-income voters often have lower turnout rates
- Barriers to voting (time off work, transportation, ID requirements)
- Access to political information and engagement

5. Solutions Could Include:
- More accountability for campaign promises
- Better voter education and access
- Stronger connection between politicians and communities
- Campaign finance reform to reduce dependency on wealthy donors

The key is ensuring genuine representation and meaningful policies rather than just electoral manipulation.
